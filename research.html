Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about peptides and research applications

Most lyophilized peptides are stable at -20°C or below for long-term storage. Short-term storage at 4°C is acceptable for a few weeks. Once reconstituted, peptide solutions should be stored at 2-8°C and used within 2-4 weeks, or aliquoted and stored at -20°C to prevent repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

First, allow the peptide vial to reach room temperature to prevent condensation. Use sterile technique and add a small volume of appropriate solvent (typically bacteriostatic water, PBS, or acetic acid solution depending on the peptide). Gently swirl the vial to dissolve - do not vortex. Once dissolved, dilute to final volume if needed. Calculate concentration based on the amount of peptide provided and final volume.

Our ≥99% purity claim is determined by analytical HPLC under UV detection at 214nm, which specifically measures the presence of the target peptide versus impurities. This includes deletion sequences, incompletely deprotected side chains, and other related impurities. Each batch comes with a Certificate of Analysis showing the actual purity value and chromatogram.
